Board Crazy Reviews The Grizzled: At Your Orders!

This week, Board Crazy reviews The Grizzled: At Your Orders!, an expansion to the co-op card game The Grizzled. At Your Orders! was designed by Fabien Riffaud and Juan Rodriguez, and published by CMON and Sweet Games. Keep reading to see what we thought of the At Your Orders! expansion.

D reviews The Grizzled: At Your Orders!

(Author’s note: this review is meant to accompany our gameplay video and will not go in-depth on the game’s rules. If you’re interested in learning how the game is played, please watch the video. It’s not bad.)

In its basic form, The Grizzled is a game that I enjoy and respect, but it has a major problem – it’s too hard. This isn’t a situation where I need to “get good” or anything like that; as it is, the game is incredibly difficult to beat. While the difficulty and high degree of randomness are thematically appropriate, given the game’s World War I setting, that doesn’t make it any less frustrating to continuously see defeat snatched from the jaws of victory because you just couldn’t get a decent combination of cards. Fortunately, The Grizzled’s first expansion, The Grizzled: At Your Orders, does a great job of re-balancing the game to make victory a much more likely outcome, while also giving the players more options to ramp up or tone down the difficulty to suit their needs. This was achieved with a couple of key changes.

Horseshoe – an easy difficulty mission card that allows you to remove two cards of the same Threat instead of one when you use your Good Luck charm.

First of all, the Support feature was tweaked. Now, when a player receives the most support, they can always reactivate their Good Luck Charm, if it has been spent. It’s not a big change in terms of rules, but it makes a world of difference. In the base game, the option of doing that is almost never beneficial, given how damaging the Hard Knocks can be. But now, you can play more fast and loose with them, and the ability to clear off even a couple more cards from No Man’s Land is huge.

The other change is the more obvious one. Instead of the mission leader simply choosing how many cards to deal out for their mission, the game now gives its players specific missions to accomplish, which come in three different difficulties: easy, normal, and hard. The rulebook also provides three different recommended balances of these mission cards to use for your game, depending on the difficulty level you’d like to play at. The hard cards are properly hard, and if you’re looking for an experience more akin to the original version, you can load up on these. However, the variety makes things more interesting, and the easy cards actually offer some boons to the players, which were almost non-existent before.

This is the best way to play the game. In fact, if you have At Your Orders, it should probably be the only way you play the game. I didn’t even mention the “Strategic Withdrawal” option or the fact that speech tokens no longer get discarded. With this expansion, the fun, core gameplay of The Grizzled still exists, but you can really tailor the game to your liking, which is fantastic. I can appreciate wanting to make a game that is properly hard, but I will always prefer a game like this that gives its players options. If you still want something that is brutally difficult, you can get that experience here, but now this is a game that can appeal to any level of player.

D’s Rating: Four and One-Half Stars out of Five.

Will reviews The Grizzled: At Your Orders!

To preface, this review is a follow-up to my prior piece on The Grizzled. I thoroughly enjoyed that game and look forward to critiquing its expansion, The Grizzled: At Your Orders!. Still, I recommend reading the review of the base game before reading this one so that everything makes the most possible sense.

That being said, there really wasn’t that much added in this expansion, which is actually a good thing. The original card game was rock-solid, but it was missing something – all it needed was a tweak or two and it was going to go from good to great. The designers, Fabien Riffaud and Juan Rodriguez, figured out what The Grizzled needed: the addition of missions. What makes these missions so effective is that they reinvigorate the already exciting gameplay, and they do this by adding a bit more thought to the proceedings. The original gameplay involved playing cards with certain weather and threat conditions on them. While that was fun, doing the same thing while also concerning yourself with conquering a fort, for example, is a lot more fun.

On the Offensive – a normal difficulty mission card that adds one active whistle Threat for the duration of that mission.

The missions aren’t perfect though, and they are limited in scope by the simple gameplay of the series. Conquering a fort might sound kind of crazy, but it really just entails a player drawing extra cards when they withdraw. In fact, most of the missions merely involve players drawing more cards or playing certain cards – yippee… Sarcasm aside, I much prefer the gameplay with the missions than without them, and some of the easier ones are such a relief to see. Also, since the leader player gets to look at two mission cards but play only one, choosing which one to activate and which one to forgo until the next round is actually really stressful. It also involves a great deal of strategy and trust in your comrades. Simple but clever gameplay such as this is starting to become a staple of this series.

In addition to all that, I also appreciate some of the minor but important alterations the designers made to the core rulebook. For instance, garnering the majority of support doesn’t just allow you to remove a couple “Hard Knocks” (status effects) from your character anymore. You can now also flip over your “Good Luck Charm” as well. Before, you had to choose between removing the status effects or getting your good luck back. Being able to do both is really useful, and this change goes a long way in balancing what once was a very difficult game.

All in all, The Grizzled: At Your Orders! is a very, very good expansion to what was already a game that I and many others thoroughly appreciated. I mean – we can actually beat it now! Huzzah! Constantly losing was becoming kind of demoralizing, so I’m very grateful for the adjustments the designers made. I really hope this isn’t the end for The Grizzled, as I would love to see where it goes next.

I give The Grizzled: At Your Orders! a: A-

Graham reviews The Grizzled: At Your Orders!

Naturally, co-op games should be pretty difficult. I mean, you have multiple (supposedly) intelligent people working together to try and defeat the game, so if it were too easy, then the game would be pointless to play. However, The Grizzled is way too challenging. I wrote about it in my review for the base game, which you can read here, but the gist is that there is too much luck involved in the gameplay, and the game doesn’t offer you enough meaningful decisions to mitigate the potential threats. As I mention in my review of the original game, there is a delicate balance that needs to be preserved because one of the best parts of The Grizzled is the fast-paced gameplay. If you give the players too many decisions to make, there is going to be an excessive amount of down time.

Small Fort – a hard difficulty mission card that forces the first player that withdraws to take two extra trials cards.

Fortunately, The Grizzled: At Your Orders! was able to solve this problem, while keeping the base game intact. There are a few main things the designers changed from the base game to the expansion. First and foremost, they allowed the leader to choose a mission that the players need to accomplish. Some of these mission cards make the game easier by providing a bonus for the entire mission; other cards add a penalty and remain in play until certain conditions are met. At the beginning of a new round, the leader gets to pick two mission cards at random and choose one to play. This card also decides the minimum amount of trial cards each player gets. The addition of these mission cards give a little more importance to the leader and also provides the players with more choices to make during the game. For example, should you try and meet a condition of the mission card, use your good luck charm, or play a threat? In the original game, your main choice basically came down to deciding which threat to play.

Another thing that At Your Orders! did was change the rules for receiving support. In the base game, when you receive support, you have to choose between getting your Good Luck charm back or removing two Hard Knocks. Replenishing your Good Luck charm becomes pointless as more Hard Knocks start showing up. Now in At Your Orders!, whenever you receive support you always get your Good Luck charm back and can also remove Hard Knocks. This makes a huge difference in terms of difficulty and also goes hand-in-hand with my point above. Now that you always get your Good Luck charm back after you have received support, you have more options to choose from on your turn. I might go as far as saying that this change makes the expansion a little too easy, but I need to play it a few more times to see if that’s really the case.

Overall, the At Your Orders! expansion is an improvement upon the base game. It gives players more decisions to make and provides the team with more bonuses to help them complete the missions. I really enjoyed The Grizzled, and the expansion did a great job of providing me with the same experience while also removing some of the luck that made the original game too tough. It’s still not worthy of two thumbs up for me, so I will give The Grizzled: At Your Orders! one thumb up.
